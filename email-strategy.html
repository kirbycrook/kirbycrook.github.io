Optimizing Your Email Capture Strategy

Strategy # 1: Email Capture on Entrance vs Checkout
Oftentimes, there may not be a lot of thought given to an email capture offer. Usually it’s something along the lines of 10% off or free shipping on your first order. If you’re showing the popup on entrance, there’s a chance that the disruption could actually be hurting conversion rate.

The first step is to test the existence of a welcome popup - does it add enough value (in collecting emails) without negatively impacting your overall conversion rate? Then, take a look at your shopping funnel in GA, especially for new users. Perhaps there’s a bigger opportunity to optimize the bottom of the funnel (initiate checkout -> transaction).

Rather than showing a discount popup when a user enters, test surprising a visitor with 10% off their order in checkout when they opt in to email marketing.

Below is an example from Home Depot - it’s subtle, but may be more effective in getting a user over the hurdle of a purchase vs showing them a similar offer right when they land on the site.

Strategy # 2: Testing Email Offer
Another approach to email capture is testing the offer itself. Is email one of your top performing channels? If so, it might be worth optimizing the signup offer. Here’s a test we ran recently - the variant offer resulted in a 97% increase in form submission rate.

Within Klaviyo (and likely most other ESPs), you can set up an AB test and tag users by their sign up source (control vs variant). From there, you'll be able to track overall revenue for each variation divided by number of email signups to get the value of one additional subscriber.

If your subscriber value holds and you double your form submission rate, that has the potential to be a huge win!
