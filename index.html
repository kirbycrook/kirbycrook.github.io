Home Away

The inspiration for the HomeAway app came out of a design sprint that took place in the Startup Instuitute course. Our goal was to take something from ideation, through the user testing phase, and into a high fidelity mockup. My app, HomeAway, is the AirBnB of all things travel related. Travelers now have access to everything they may need without the hassle of packing it all with them. Lendors make money renting out items that previously were simply taking up space when not being used. Whether it's a bike in Copenhagen or a pair of skis in Vail, HomeAway provides a platform to connect lendors and renters all across the world.

The design sprint was done individually, so I took the concept through the entire design process. I began with a Who-Do chart to determine who I thought would be the various customer segments, and how they would act. I came up with three users with three different motivations. The information I had compiled in the chart was influenced by either assumptions or facts; my next step was to lay out a number of assumptions, facts, and questions that were associated with what I had so far. I then developed a problem statement and narrowed my target market down to a single user persona. With a more concrete idea of who my user would be, I started sketching, wireframing, and developing a user flow.

As I moved to more hi-fi mockups, I had a number of goals in mind that were influencing my decisions. Traveling can sometimes be a hassle; I wanted to make the user experience as low-stress and fluid as possible. Allowing a viewer to search for equipment prior to logging in or signing up gives him or her the chance to see the inventory with minimal click-thrus. I took a mobile-first design approach; due to the array of features that could be included, designing mobile first allowed me to assess the most pertinant features and progressively enhance as the screen size increased. G-RIDE Redesign

G-Ride is an eco-friendly ride sharing application that aims at raising global eco-consciousness and lowering the carbon footprint. Their message was fairly clear, but the delivery was not effective. The current version of the site can be seen below www.gogride.com

The lack of brand identity led me to develop a logo, consistent style, and color scheme for the webpage. I referenced G-Ride's current site for necessary materials, content, and pages in order to develop a user flow. I created a logo that would give the viewer a basic understanding of the business, even without surrounding context. The typeface reflects the innovative, modernistic brand that G-Ride is hoping to develop.

The site is currently being used to collect the information of prospective users and raise brand awareness; with that in mind, I prioritized the call to action on multiple pages, and reduced the clutter of the home page. The resulting webpage is one that has strong brand identity, streamlines the user experience, and establishes a consistency that was previously missing.
